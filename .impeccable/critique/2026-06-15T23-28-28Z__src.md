## Overall Impression

Pazant reads as a credible, governance-forward federal advisory practice — not generic SaaS. The institutional palette, serif/sans pairing, and recent layout pass (split grounding/callout, unified capability panel, fluid section rhythm) show real craft. The single biggest opportunity is **cohesion**: unify voice (I/we), hero treatment across pages, and reduce credential/quote repetition so "selective, high-trust" feels earned rather than asserted.

## What's Working

- **Institutional palette discipline** — Navy/teal/gold roles are consistent; gold stays accent-only; muted text contrast was tightened in polish pass.
- **About page hero** — Split layout with contained navy credentials panel is the strongest first impression on the site; aligns with "Institutional Briefing Room" north star.
- **Progressive disclosure on Services** — Accordion keeps six services scannable without a wall of cards; deliverables appear on demand.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Voice inconsistency ("I" vs "We")**
- **Why it matters:** Homepage NarrativeSection speaks in first person ("I help leaders…") while adjacent sections, CTAs, and the rest of the site use "We/Pazant Consulting." Executive readers notice; it undermines selective-practice credibility.
- **Fix:** Pick one voice for the marketing site (recommend third-person institutional or first-person founder-only on About). Align NarrativeSection copy with Section headers and footer.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable clarify`

**[P1] Hero inconsistency across pages**
- **Why it matters:** Home uses a plain 50/50 stock split; About uses a contained asymmetric hero with credentials panel; Services/Contact use PageIntro bands. Returning visitors feel like three different sites.
- **Fix:** Improve one hero at a time with user approval (Home OR Services next — do not bulk-redesign). Borrow About's credential anchoring or typographic weight without copying layout verbatim.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable layout Hero` or `$impeccable bolder` (scoped to one page)

**[P2] Credential repetition fatigue**
- **Why it matters:** SBA Advisor / SBLO / MIT Sloan appear in CredentialStrip (Home), AboutHero panel, GroundingGrid lists, StrengthsBrief groups, and Services foundation — five surfaces before footer. Contradicts "selective" positioning.
- **Fix:** Show full credential block once per page max; elsewhere use one-line proof points or link to About.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable distill`

**[P2] Stock photography undermines trust**
- **Why it matters:** Generic boardroom towers and stock headshot on About founder block signal template site, not selective advisory. High-trust federal buyers look for real founder presence.
- **Fix:** Replace with Alecia's headshot and one institutional photo (briefing room, documents, federal context — not skyline cliché).
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable delight` (after assets supplied)

**[P2] Homepage cognitive length**
- **Why it matters:** Seven sections before footer; StrengthsBrief largely duplicates Services "why choose" content. Squint test: everything is medium importance.
- **Fix:** Cut or merge StrengthsBrief into infrastructure section; tighten ServiceGrid to 3+featured or link-only on home.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable distill` or `$impeccable layout`

## Persona Red Flags

"Book a Consultation" appears 4+ times with different destinations (contact anchor vs mailto). Mobile sheet nav doesn't show `aria-current` active state like desktop.

About hero is closer to the promise; Home oversells volume.
